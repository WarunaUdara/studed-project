# Target Audience

> [!abstract] Summary
> StudEd is strictly targeted at Sri Lankan schools, serving students from **Grade 1 through A/L**. The platform must accommodate varying cognitive levels, language preferences (especially Sinhala), and curriculum standards.

## Primary User Groups

### 1. Students (The Learners)

The core consumers of the platform. They engage with Courses, complete Lessons, and progress through Waves.

| Persona | Age Range | Grade | Key Needs |
|---------|-----------|-------|-----------|
| **Young Learners** | 6–10 | Grade 1–5 | Highly visual, audio-rich content, simple drag-and-drop interactions, gamified rewards |
| **Junior Students** | 11–14 | Grade 6–9 | Structured progression, varied question types (MCQ, fill-in-blank), competitive leaderboards |
| **O/L Candidates** | 15–16 | Grade 10–11 | Exam-focused evaluation, revision tools, timed quizzes, proficiency tracking |
| **A/L Students** | 17–19 | Advanced Level | Deep subject mastery, complex interactive exercises, high-stakes evaluation, peer competition |

### 2. Educators (The Creators)

Teachers, tutors, and content creators who build Courses, Lessons, and Waves using the [[MDX Editor|editor]].

- Must be able to create content in **Sinhala** and English.
- Need an **AI-assisted workflow** to speed up content generation.
- Require an intuitive, drag-and-drop interface (e.g., [[Puck Research|Puck]]).

### 3. School Administrators

Principals, department heads, or coordinators who may purchase subscriptions on behalf of students.

- Bulk enrollment and license management.
- Reporting on student progress across classes.
- Visibility into platform usage and performance.

### 4. Parents (Indirect Stakeholders)

- Monitor child progress (if parent dashboard is built).
- Motivated by leaderboard standings and proficiency achievements.

## Language Considerations

> [!warning] Critical Requirement
> The platform **must fully support the Sinhala language** across:
> - The [[Learn Component|Learn]] and [[Evaluate Component|Evaluate]] content.
> - The [[MDX Editor]] interface and AI prompts.
> - UI labels, notifications, and gamification messages.
> - Database storage and search (Unicode/Sinhala collation).

## Accessibility & Inclusivity

- Mobile-first design for students with limited desktop access.
- Low-bandwidth mode for areas with poor connectivity.
- Clear, legible fonts supporting Sinhala script.
- Audio narration options for younger learners.
